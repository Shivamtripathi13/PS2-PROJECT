Computer Science and Engineering.

Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) deals with the study of computers and computer systems, and is one of the most important professions in the world. Computer systems have become a ubiquitous and integral part of almost everything we do today, from entertainment to medicine and more. The real-world applications of the computer engineering course have grown exponentially over the past decade, which is why there has been a steady increase in the demand for well-trained computer science graduates.

At BMU, the students are trained with different subjects including data warehousing, Cloud computing, big data analytics, computer graphics and animation, NLP & Text Analytics, Information Retrieval, Host Computer Security and more.

The B.Tech CSE programme is constantly evolving and diversifying, which is leading to the creation of a range of new opportunities in areas such as:

Artificial Intelligence, Big Data Analytics, Cyber Security, Internet of Things, Data Science, Machine Learning,

Electronics & Communication.

India is on the verge of becoming the manufacturing hub of consumer electronics products and in the era of deployment of 5G, the Electronics and Communications Engineering (ECE) is one of the most promising branches of engineering study. Electronics and communications engineers find themselves in leading positions in research, design, development and testing of the electronic equipment used in various systems and communication networks. In the present era of Internet of Things (IoT), Industry 4.0, Big data, and nanotechnology, the ECE branch plays the anchor role by bridging all branches including Computer Science and Engineering, Mechanical engineering, and Applied Sciences. This is the cause of ever-increasing in demand of ECE degree for the last two decades.

This branch primarily deals with areas such as:

Nanoelectronics, VLSI , Robotics and Industrial automation, Embedded system design, Advanced communication system and networks, Wireless communications, Internet of Things and Big data analytics, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering is amongst the most diversified and versatile engineering branch and plays an important role in all aspects of human life. A Mechanical Engineer develops products by taking an idea, developing it into a functional prototype, performing engineering analysis (structural, thermal, vibrational, static, dynamic etc.), applying manufacturing technologies & production processes for the required form and fit required to perform the intended function.

This branch primarily deals with areas such as:

Automotive systems, Fuels, Combustion, Internal combustion engine, Fluid mechanics, Hydraulics and Pneumatics, Thermodynamics, Heat transfer, Energy conversion and HVAC, Control of dynamic systems, Design of mechanical systems, Manufacturing processes and systems, Production technology, Industrial Engineering, Tribology, Materials behaviour and processing, Composites, Robotics and Automation, Mechatronics, Computer-Aided Design and Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAD/CAM), Computer-Aided Engineering for FEA and Simulation
